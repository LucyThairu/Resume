User-centered design: This is the foundation of UX design, and it involves understanding the needs of users and designing products that meet those needs.
Design thinking: This is a creative problem-solving approach that can be used to come up with innovative solutions to user problems.
Visual design: This is the art of creating visually appealing and user-friendly interfaces. It includes typography, color, layout, and other elements of design.
Prototyping and wireframing: This is the process of creating low-fidelity models of your designs to test and iterate on your ideas.
Research: UX designers need to be able to conduct user research to understand the needs of their users. This can involve conducting surveys, interviews, and usability testing.
Testing: UX designers need to be able to test their designs to make sure they are meeting the needs of their users. This can involve conducting usability testing and A/B testing.
